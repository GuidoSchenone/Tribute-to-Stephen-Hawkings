Stephen Hawking

Renowned physicist and cosmologist

Born: January 8, 1942

Died: March 14, 2018

Known for his work on black holes and theoretical physics

Contributions to Science

  • Hawking Radiation: Stephen Hawking's theory of Hawking radiation explained how black holes emit radiation and gradually lose mass.
  • Theoretical Cosmology: Hawking made significant contributions to our understanding of the universe's origins and evolution, incorporating quantum mechanics and general relativity.
  • Singularity Theorems: Collaborating with Roger Penrose, Hawking formulated important theorems on the existence of singularities in gravitational collapse.
  • Books and Public Outreach: Hawking's books, such as "A Brief History of Time," made complex scientific ideas accessible to the general public, inspiring interest in cosmology and physics.
